How it runs

From trigger to outcome, in four moves

Every live flow walks the same path. The first and last moves are where the value compounds: the trigger reads real customer events, and the outcome feeds back into a smarter plan.

1

Trigger fires

A real moment starts the flow: a first visit, a regular gone quiet for 45 days, a new review, a season.

Needs POS, reservations, and review feeds.
2

Customer enrolls

The customer enters the flow and the engine tracks where they are: which step, when waits end, how branches decide.

Needs enrollment + step state.
3

Work gets delivered

Each step runs. Software sends and tags on its own. Human work becomes a task for the right vendor, or the owner.

Needs delivery adapters + the marketplace.
4

Outcome is captured

What happened gets logged and tied back to covers and spend, so Results are real and the next plan is sharper.

Needs outcome capture. This is the moat.

For the team & investors

The model underneath, and what's left to build

The owner-facing page above hides the mechanics. Here is the revenue model, the take-rates, why it fits this market, and the honest build sequence.

Layer 1 · Core

Subscription

Tiered SaaS gating locations, automation volume, and access. The predictable ARR and the exit-multiple driver.

Free → $149 → $399 / mo
Layer 2 · Expansion

Marketplace take-rate

Owners hire vetted vendors inside a flow. Funds escrowed, released on approval. Two-sided network effect + more ROI data.

~15% service fee
Layer 3 · Expansion

Done-for-you + ads

Productized DFY deliverables (fixed price, never hourly) for high-willingness owners, plus a fee on managed ad spend.

Per deliverable · ~10% ad mgmt

Why subscription-led is optimal for restaurants

  • Predictability beats everything. A flat monthly number fits a tight restaurant P&L. Usage-led billing would scare them off.
  • Free Starter kills the cold start. Zero risk to begin lands every restaurant in the data flywheel, where the moat is built.
  • The marketplace fee feels fair, because the owner sees the vendor's price and the fee is modest. Escrow-on-approval removes the classic agency fear.
  • DFY captures the high spenders without forcing everyone into a retainer they don't want.
  • For exit: recurring subscription is the multiple driver; marketplace and DFY stack on as expansion revenue and a network-effect story.
