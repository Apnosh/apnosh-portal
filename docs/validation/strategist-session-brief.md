# Strategist Alignment Session — Brief

**Use:** before the goal layer build. One 30-min session with one strategist. Mark runs it.
**Goal:** test whether the goal-led product reframe matches how the team actually operates.

---

## Setup (5 min)

Open with context, no defense:

> "We've been rethinking how Apnosh is positioned and operates. Before we build any of it, I want your gut on whether it matches reality. Tell me where it's wrong. I won't be defensive."

Hand them `PRODUCT-SPEC.md` printed out, or share screen.

Tell them:
- This is rough draft. Their reaction shapes the next round.
- They won't hurt your feelings. Honesty is the deliverable.
- This is not announcing a change to their workflow. It's testing whether a possible change matches what they already do.

---

## Walk-through (10 min)

Cover three sections of the spec, in this order:

### Section 1: The 8 goals (catalog)

Read the 8 goals aloud. Then ask:

> **"Which one is the most common thing your clients actually want? Pick the top 3 in your head."**

Listen for: which they pick, and whether they pick using your wording or their own. If they say "well I'd describe it as X" — that's the signal. The catalog should match their language.

> **"Are any of these goals you've never heard a client articulate that way?"**

Listen for: goals that feel theoretical to them. If a goal isn't in their head, it might not be in owners' heads either.

> **"Is there a goal that comes up all the time that's missing from this list?"**

Listen for: anything new. Especially if they hesitate before saying "no, that's pretty much it."

---

### Section 2: The cultural principle (decline-to-sell)

Read this aloud:

> *"We show every owner what we know about their situation. We don't tell them what to do. We don't refuse to work with them. But we won't sell them services we don't believe will work — we have the conversation, recommend the foundation, and let them choose to build it."*

Then ask:

> **"Have you ever sold a client a service you didn't think they were ready for? What was the situation?"**

Listen for: the war stories. They probably exist. The presence of these stories is itself a validation that this principle is needed.

> **"Would you have refused if the system told you not to? Or would you have wanted the autonomy to override?"**

Listen for: tension between system-as-rule and strategist-as-judgment. The right answer is "the system flags it; I have the conversation; I decide" — if their answer is "I want full autonomy" or "I want the system to just block it," we have a culture conversation.

---

### Section 3: The strategist console + goal layer

Show them `/admin/console` (use real data; you've been using it for a few days by this point).

> **"If this had a column for goal-progress added — would that help you act on it? What would be noise?"**

Listen for: what signals they want and don't want. Their model of "what matters" might differ from the score formula (overdue × 3 + bad reviews × 2 + tokens × 1).

> **"If a client's quarterly goal review was a 30-min call with you, generated brief from Apnosh — would you do that for every client?"**

Listen for: capacity concerns ("at 20 clients that's 20 calls a quarter — feasible?"), workflow concerns ("when does it happen in my week?"), enthusiasm signals.

---

## Listen for (across all sections)

| Signal | What it might mean |
|---|---|
| Strategist describes a goal in different words | Adopt their wording |
| Strategist hesitates on a goal | Maybe drop it or rephrase |
| War stories around bad-fit clients | Validates decline-to-sell principle |
| "I want full autonomy" or "the system should just block it" | Culture conversation needed before build |
| "I'd want X column too" on console | Add to backlog |
| "I don't have time for that" on Q-review | Real capacity constraint, factor into design |
| Defensiveness | They feel threatened — listen carefully, this is the risky reaction |
| Enthusiasm | Could be authentic or could be flattery — verify by asking "what would you change?" |

---

## What NOT to do

- **Don't sell.** This is not a buy-in meeting. Listen, take notes, follow up if needed.
- **Don't defend.** When they say "I don't think that's how it works," your only response is "tell me more."
- **Don't promise.** Their input may or may not change the spec. Don't commit to changes in the room.
- **Don't argue.** Note disagreements, move on. Synthesize after.
- **Don't pitch the goal layer build.** They're not approving a build; they're stress-testing the spec.

---

## After the session

Write 1-page synthesis. Three sections:

1. **What confirms the spec** — points where their gut matched the framing
2. **What revises the spec** — places where their language, workflow, or judgment differs from what we wrote
3. **What's still ambiguous** — things they couldn't answer or punted on

Send to me. We update the spec or kill assumptions before the goal layer build.

---

## Time budget

- 5 min setup
- 10 min walk-through
- 10 min open conversation
- 5 min wrap

If it runs over to 45 min because the conversation is rich — let it. If you're done in 20 because they have nothing to say — that's also a signal (they may be checking out, or the spec might be obvious to them, or the questions weren't sharp enough).

---

## Pick the right strategist

The first session should be with a strategist who:
- Has been at Apnosh long enough to have war stories
- Has clients across multiple shapes (ideally: a Stage 1 neighborhood spot AND a Stage 2 multi-loc)
- Isn't afraid to push back on you

Avoid: the most senior person (they might agree out of seniority diplomacy), the newest hire (no war stories yet).
