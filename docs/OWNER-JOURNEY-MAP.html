PART 2 · THE WORKBENCH

Where the plan is built

The briefing on your phone does not appear by magic. It comes from four screens in the Workbench. Your strategist drives these screens; you drop in to set goals and approve the direction. Each one answers a plain question, and each is made to read like a strategist's worksheet, not a database. Here is what each is for, what's on screen, and exactly when you'd open it.

How to read a flow, top to bottom

  • Trigger: the moment it starts. Here it just runs every week. Others start when someone visits, orders, or a date arrives.
  • Stages: the steps of the customer's journey: Awareness (get noticed), Decision (help them choose you), Loyalty / Advocacy (bring them back, get referrals).
  • Services: the actual work in each stage (a post, a Google update), with the channels it runs on as colored tags.
  • Vendor: who delivers it. "Pick vendor" until you choose.
  • Right away: the wait between steps. Change it to wait days or weeks.

When you'd use it

  • Set and forget: build a welcome flow once; every new diner gets it automatically.
  • Start from a template: pick a ready-made recipe tailored to your concept, then tweak.
  • Add a tactic: a campaign needs a step, so you drop a service into the right stage.
  • Hand off: assign a vendor so your strategist or a freelancer runs it.

PLAIN WORDS

The jargon, translated

Every term an owner meets in the Workbench, in one line.

Brief
A short write-up of your restaurant and its challenge. Think: the situation in a paragraph.
Goal
A number you want to hit by a date. Think: the finish line.
Strategy bet
A big move you're betting will work. Think: the plan to win.
Campaign Plan
A push with a start, end, and budget. Think: one bar on your calendar.
Flow Flows
A set of steps that runs on its own. Think: a recipe that cooks itself.
Trigger Flows
The moment a flow starts. Think: "when this happens, begin."
Stage Flows
Where a customer is: Awareness, Decision, Loyalty, Advocacy. Think: steps from stranger to regular.
Service
One piece of actual work, like a post or a review reply. Think: a task.
Channel
Where the work shows up: Instagram, Email, Google. Think: the place customers see it.
Vendor
Who does the work: Apnosh, a freelancer, an agency, or you. Think: who's on the job.
