# Apnosh — Goal-Led Marketing for Restaurants

**Status:** Foundational. Read this before designing features, hiring, pitching customers, or scoping work.
**Last reviewed:** 2026-05-08.

---

## What we are

Apnosh is the marketing partner that **figures out what each restaurant actually needs and only sells them that.** Existing vendors push channels (social, SEO, email) and let owners guess what fits. Most owners guess wrong, pay for things that don't move the needle, and learn nothing in the process. Apnosh is the anti-pattern: a strategist + AI engine that diagnoses goals, prescribes the marketing mix, executes it, and reports against the goal — not the channel.

Owners get sales growth they can attribute. Strategists get leverage from a productized agency. Apnosh gets a moat that compounds: every quarter, the playbooks get sharper.

---

## Who we serve

Restaurant owners who *know* marketing matters but can't operate it correctly. They've tried things (an agency, a freelancer, "doing it themselves"), been disappointed, and don't trust the vendor ecosystem. They have **$300–$800/month** to spend, want results in **plain English**, and want to talk to a real human who knows their business.

**Not** every restaurant. Below ~$300/mo, the human-in-the-loop economics don't work. Above ~$2,500/mo, the customer is sophisticated enough to assemble specialists themselves. Apnosh is the middle.

**Not** enterprise (yet). Multi-region chains and franchisors require different surfaces. The data model leaves room; the product doesn't build for them in 2026.

## How it works

Three artifacts make Apnosh work:

1. **Restaurant shape.** Captured at onboarding. Four dimensions: footprint (single-neighborhood / single-destination / multi-local / multi-regional / mobile / ghost), concept (QSR / fast casual / casual / fine / bar / cafe / mobile / delivery / catering), customer mix (local-repeat / local-destination / tourist / regional / B2B), digital maturity (nascent / basic / active / sophisticated). Owner-supplied; strategist sanity-checks.

2. **Active goals.** Owner picks **up to 3** from the catalog of 8 (below). Each is prioritized 1, 2, 3. If they can't pick, smart defaults derive from shape. Reviewed every 90 days with the strategist.

3. **Playbooks.** For every goal, Apnosh has a recommended service mix. The mix adapts based on shape. The strategist executes the mix, overrides when context demands, and logs why. Overrides + outcomes feed back into the playbooks every quarter.

The dashboard, the strategist console, the service catalog, the deliverable tracking — everything reorganizes around active goals.

## The 8 goals (in owner voice)

| Goal | Drives sales through | Primary lever |
|---|---|---|
| **More foot traffic** | Volume of new customers | GBP, local SEO, location-targeted ads |
| **My regulars come back more often** | Visit frequency | Email/SMS, loyalty, daypart content |
| **More online orders** | Digital channel revenue | Delivery-app optimization, social CTAs |
| **More reservations** | Booked covers | Reservation funnel, OpenTable/Resy, content |
| **Better online reputation** | Trust → conversion | Review fetch + response, reputation campaigns |
| **Be known as the spot for ___** | Branded demand | Content engine, social storytelling, influencers |
| **Fill my slow times** | Daypart utilization | Targeted promos, daypart content |
| **Grow catering / private events** | B2B and event revenue | B2B SEO, LinkedIn, catering landing pages |

Each goal has a measurable signal Apnosh tracks. Goals are reviewed quarterly.

What's **not** in the catalog: "more sales" (translated to specific levers above), "open another location" (business decision; we support readiness work but don't sell against it), "sell the business" (corporate decision; same), "build a brand" (translated to "be known as the spot for ___"), "compete with [X]" (collapses into reputation + brand).

## The cultural principle

> **We show every owner what we know about their situation. We don't tell them what to do. We don't refuse to work with them. But we won't sell them services we don't believe will work — we have the conversation, recommend the foundation, and let them choose to build it.**

In practice: when an owner sets a goal that doesn't fit their shape (e.g. "open another location" with $300/mo budget and 6-month-old single store), Apnosh shows the math ("this typically requires ~$2,500/mo and 18 months at current scale"), declines to sell franchise-readiness services at the wrong stage, and offers the foundation work instead (reputation, repeatable customer base, brand documentation). Owner chooses.

This is the operating practice of "anti-rip-off." Memorize it. Every strategist, every onboarding email, every sales call reflects it.

## What Apnosh is NOT

- **Not a channel-first SaaS.** We don't sell "social media management" or "SEO services" as standalone products. Channels serve goals; goals lead.
- **Not a self-serve product.** Strategists are the moat. The platform supports strategists; it doesn't replace them.
- **Not a marketplace.** Service requests go to Apnosh's internal team, not a vendor pool. We are a productized agency, not a two-sided platform.
- **Not an operations platform.** No inventory, staffing, scheduling, finance. POS data may inform marketing (audience segments) but Apnosh is the marketing layer, full stop.
- **Not a generalist.** Restaurants only. Every word of copy, every playbook, every onboarding question is tuned to restaurants.
- **Not for enterprise yet.** Multi-region chains, franchisors, 20+ location operators are out of scope through 2026. Independent operators and small multi-location are the customer.

## The moat

Three layers, in order of compounding strength:

1. **Trust** — earned by declining to sell what isn't ready. Existing vendors don't, and won't, because their incentives don't allow it.
2. **Strategist-in-the-loop** — every recommendation is a real human's judgment, not pure automation. Trust requires accountability requires a human.
3. **Playbook IP** — every quarter, overrides + outcomes refine the matrix of (goal × shape → recommended mix). At year 2, the playbooks are operationally valuable to no one but Apnosh. That's the durable moat.

The first two layers are operating culture. The third is data + ritual + team commitment. Without the third, this is a great agency. With it, this is a great company.

## What this means for what we build

- The **goal layer** is the next thing built. Every Q2 feature (Meta Ads, Klaviyo, advanced SEO) plugs into it as a service that serves specific goals.
- **Onboarding** is the most important UX. The goal-selection screen is the moat in product form — the moment owners learn marketing while choosing.
- **Quarterly review** is the heartbeat. Build the product around the rhythm, not the dashboard.
- **Strategist console** measures goal progress, surfaces tensions, flags shape-goal mismatches. The dashboard supports the conversation; the conversation drives the work.
